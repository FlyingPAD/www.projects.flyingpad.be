Leap Year

The Earth takes approximately 365.25 days to orbit the Sun, which is known as a solar year. However, a calendar year has 365 days.

The remaining 0.25 days accumulate each year, which means that after four years, we have an extra day (0.25 days x 4 = 1 day).

To compensate for this difference, we add a day to the month of February every four years, making it 29 days instead of 28.

This is called a leap year.

A year is a leap year if it is divisible by 4. However, if the year is divisible by 100, it must also be divisible by 400. For example, 2000 is a leap year, but 1900 is not.
