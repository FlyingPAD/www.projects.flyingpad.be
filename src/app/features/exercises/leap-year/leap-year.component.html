A leap year has an extra day, February 29, to keep the calendar aligned with the Earth's orbit around the Sun, which takes about 365.25 days.

A year is a leap year if it is divisible by 4. However, if the year is divisible by 100, it must also be divisible by 400. For example, 2000 is a leap year, but 1900 is not.
